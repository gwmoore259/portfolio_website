I have found that life is rarely ever a straight line. The journey to at last figure out who we are - what makes us come alive - sometimes is inherent with stops, detours, and in my case, completely turning around.

A return to college for a second degree, in graphic design nonetheless, was never part of the plan. Yet it is in creating beautiful things where I truly feel my purpose; and this chapter in my story is just beginning.

What follows here are the products of my return to design and my desire to keep growing and improving as a designer. After all, any destination worth reaching is certainly worth the journey one must embark on to get there. Now let's explore!

Copywriting

“A flame from a candle is only passed by way of touching the wick of the lit candle to that of the unlit one. Much the same, our light is most effectively shared with others when we get close to them, allowing our lives to touch theirs in a meaningful way. We must care for them — genuinely so — interacting with them in such a way that they might see how we live our lives and see something missing in their own.”

“Light is unique in its ability to be felt, to evoke a warmth that can be tangibly perceived. I would venture to say that a strong correlation exists between how deeply we will enter into the lives of those we aim to share our light with and the extent to which our light is felt by them. When a friend’s car breaks down and he has no way to get to work, there is a measurable difference between giving him a couple dollars for a one-way bus pass, giving him a ride, and giving him your car for the week until his is repaired. Now granted, few of us have the resources necessary to lend out all of our vehicles to those who need them, but my argument remains: our light can be felt the warmest when it involves real sacrifice to us to share it.”

- Excerpt from Chapter 3: The Light
